# Project Specification: German Publishing & Neoliberal Moral Order

## 🎯 Vision
This PhD research project investigates the "nexus between traditional religions with economically driven ventures in the contemporary German publishing landscape" through the lens of **Critical Theory** and **LDA Topic Modeling**. The goal is to uncover "latent thematic structures" across a heterogeneous corpus of text, specifically focusing on how market logic has redesigned faith and ethics.

## 👥 Stakeholders
- **PhD Researcher**: Primary investigator and project lead.
- **Antigravity**: Chief Technical Architect and Implementer.

## 🛠️ Objectives
1.  **LDA Mapping**: Use Latent Dirichlet Allocation to map discursive structures in contemporary German publishing.
2.  **Institutional Analysis**: Case studies of major houses: Bertelsmann, Holtzbrinck, Suhrkamp, etc.
3.  **Theoretical Bridge**: Synthesize computational results with Critical Theory concepts (neoliberal spirituality, commodification).
4.  **Doctoral Documentation**: Produce analysis chapters with academic-grade citations (APA/Chicago) and visualizations.

## 📐 Methodology
- **Core Engine**: `python-gensim` pipeline.
- **Preprocessing**: High-fidelity German NLP (Lemmatization, POS filtering, bigram detection).
- **Interpretation**: Socio-historical analysis using Critical Theory frameworks.

## 📦 Constraints & Requirements
- **Language**: Core corpus is German.
- **Quality**: Must use `spacy` large model (`de_core_news_lg`) for lemmatization accuracy.
- **Workflow**: Adhere to the **Get Shit Done (GSD)** methodology for spec-driven, context-engineered developments.
