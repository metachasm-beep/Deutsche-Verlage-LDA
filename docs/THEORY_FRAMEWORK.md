# Theory Framework: Voices on Religion in German Publishing

This document maps the identified publishing categories to the theoretical archetypes for the final PhD analysis.

## 🏛️ 1. Traditional / Ecclesiastical Voice
This voice focuses on institutional theology, dogma, and communal religious life.
- **Key Indicators**: *Kirche, Gott, Seelsorge, Liturgie, Gemeinde.*
- **Core Publishers**: Herder, Butzon & Bercker, Evangelische Verlagsanstalt (EVA).
- **Discursive Role**: Maintains traditional moral order and institutional authority.

## 📈 2. Neoliberal / Entrepreneurial Voice
This voice represents the "nexus between traditional religion and economically driven ventures."
- **Key Indicators**: *Selbstoptimierung, Erfolg, Achtsamkeit, Markt-Sinn, Spirituelle Ökonomie.*
- **Core Imprints**: Gütersloher Verlagshaus (Bertelsmann), Ariston (RH), Scorpios.
- **Discursive Role**: Commodification of faith for individual market performance.

## ⚖️ 3. Critical / Secular-Humanist Voice
This voice analyzes religion through the lens of power, history, and secular critique.
- **Key Indicators**: *Atheismus, Aufklärung, Machtkritik, Säkularismus, Humanismus.*
- **Core Publishers**: Alibri, Suhrkamp (Philosophy series), Rowohlt.
- **Discursive Role**: Challenges the "Neoliberal Moral Order" and institutional dogma.
